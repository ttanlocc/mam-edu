### Invariants baked into this map
- **Every cell ∈ material-inventory.md** (real downloaded archive). No Cambridge-book / Simon / Hancock / Collins refs (not downloaded).
- **ALL 52 weeks contain ZERO Writing work** — no Writing block, no essay/Task-1/Task-2/marking hidden in other skills. The NB Writing course + marking loop are reserved for next year (giai đoạn 2).
- **P3–4 = Speaking-forward, not L/R-light.** Speaking owns the biggest block (85′/day) and is pushed 7.0→8.0 (P3)→8.5 (P4). L & R keep 65–70′/day of real full-mock volume — the *maintenance-drift* pitfall (assuming 9.0 is "done") is the top risk of the back half.
- **Feedback timing:** iTk diagnostic w4 · iTk 1×/wk w5–13 · iTk 2×/wk w14–52 · scored S mocks w13/w20/w26/w35/w39/w47. From zero, Speaking cannot self-grade — the tutor loop is load-bearing. **NO Writing marking anywhere.**
- **L/R banks reused across the year**: the full-test banks (NB-R10 Cambridge/BC, NP1–26) carry the w1–26 grind; P3–4 reuse NP1–9 (fresh eyes ≥6 months later), RtI1–9 and the BC bank for full-mock volume. Reuse/review acceptable — the point is precision, not novelty.
- **Speaking is the year's star skill** — its 8.5 target leans on 12 months of continuity (receptive 9.0 base + tutor loop). Fluency + pronunciation-feature work never pauses, even in test week.
- **`[opt]` gap-fillers** (advanced pronunciation course, extra Speaking Part 1/3 material) appear only as optional accelerators — the plan is runnable 100% on the downloaded archive + iTk/ELSA. **Writing (0→7.0) + full overall 8.5 = next year.**
